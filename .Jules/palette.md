## 2024-04-02 - Custom Keyboard Accessibility for Interactive Divs
**Learning:** In this application, interactive quiz options (`.option`) are implemented as `div` elements instead of native form controls (like `<input type="radio">` or `<button>`). Because they lack native semantics and keyboard handling, they are completely inaccessible to keyboard users and screen readers by default. Relying solely on `onclick` events leaves out a significant portion of users.
**Action:** Always ensure that custom interactive elements acting as buttons or radios have appropriate ARIA roles (`role="radio"`, `role="radiogroup"`), focus management (`tabindex="0"`), accessible state communication (`aria-checked`), and explicit keyboard event listeners (handling `Enter` and `Space` keys) to replicate native control behavior.
